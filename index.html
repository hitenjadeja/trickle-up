Chapter 02 / Two kinds of money

There are two kinds of money. Almost everyone only has one of them.

Wages are what you trade time for. Assets are what trade time for you. The first runs out, the second compounds. Most of the country lives on the first.

Chapter 04 / Debt is an asset

Your debt is somebody else's income.

Mortgages, credit cards, car loans, student loans. Every monthly payment you make is a return on someone else's wealth.

Chapter 05 / The pull-away

Wages grow at 1%. Assets grow at 8%. Drag the years and watch what happens.

A small annual gap looks harmless. Compounded over a working lifetime, it is the entire story of modern inequality.
